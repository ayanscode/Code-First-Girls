Physical self care involves taking care of your physical body. This is as simple as staying fit and mantaining healthy habits to ensure that your body functions properly. Withouut practicing physical self care, it's easy to fall into a slump, which can take a toll on your mental and emotional wellbeing too. Luckily, we've come up with some tips on how to practice physical self care.

Eating healthy

Contrary to popular belief, this does not mean buying the entire green section of Whole Foods. However, it is important that you eat a balanced diet for the sake of your health. Not only will you be fuelling your superiority complex by eating more greens, but you also will be getting your 5 a day. In addition, your body will thank you for taking the supplements to provide you with the vitamins it needs.

Remember though, it's all about balance.

Eating healthily should not be about limiting yourself. By all means eat that chocolate bar or that 6pc chicken nugget meal. The main thing is that you incorporate the necessary components of the beloved eatwell plate. And that includes drinking enough water too.

Physical Activity

This can be in any form you like: go for a run, walk or jog. Do a yoga class, or maybe some gymnastics (be careful!) Ride a bike. Do a Chloe Ting Workout. Maybe you're not into the whole fit girl aesthetic. That's perfectly fine! You could even dance to your favourite song and poorly attempt to copy the choreo. So long as you get your heart pumping for 20-25 minutes a day :).

Physical activity is not just to attain whichever body goals you have, but it is a known fact that exercise causes your body to release endorphins as well as increase your serotonin levels, boosting your mood. What's more, regular exercise can also help you to sleep better, reduce the chance of developing various health problems such as high blood pressure and stroke. It's a win win.

(bonus idea!) cuddle

Or invest in a pet, pets are really good to cuddle where humans are low in stock. And if you cannot get a pet, costco and ikea do really cute teddy bear plushies :)
